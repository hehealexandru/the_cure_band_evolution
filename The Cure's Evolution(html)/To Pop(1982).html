1982:To Pop...

Depending on which interview with Smith you want to belive, the song was either a musical experiment, a label enforced hit attempt, or based on more recent interviews, a concious decision to go against the all-consuming rock of Pornography .

The only way I could get Cure music played on the radio was to make something that was radio-friendly so 'Let's Go To Bed' was entierly designed [to do that]. It was the only time we've ever done it, to get us played on the radio, particularly in America. And It worked!
Robert Smith: Yahoo! Entertainment (July 2018)

The original demo dubbed Temptation 2 doesn't sound too dissimilar to what the band had made on Pornography :thickly rythmic with a synth topping, the bassline and percussion seeming like a faster She's Lost Control by Joy Division .

In its fully realized form Let's Go To Bed 's funky synthpop and all too blunt satirical lyrics seemed aimed at sending up Duran Duran . When asked about his greatest musical nemesis, in 2017 Smith stated:

It was generally Duran Duran which is really sad because they loved us, and they used to come to our shows. But they represented everything we hated: the whole glamorous 80s, consumer bullshit; this horrorshow that we were up against.
Robert Smith: The Guardian (June 2018)

Musically, Let's Go To Bed may not be The Cure's best work, and chart wise only reach number 44 in the UK, but it's video featuring the band, now a two-piece, dancing about like a drunken Tears For Fears , and their willingness to experiment with the poppier new wave side of music would serve them well for the rest of the decade. Two other pop focused tracks followed in 1983: the New Order indebted The Walk and the Aristocats jazz of The Lovecats ,all later released as a compilation called The Japanese Whispers .
This new wave direction will make them progressively bigger worldwide, each new single would be paired with a Tim Pope directed promotional clip, which in turn would be broadcast on MTV and increase the band's profile especially in the States.

The next few years would produce an enviable collection of singles: In Between Days ,Close To Me , a re-release of Boys Don't Cry , Why Can't I Be You , and finally their biggest hit thus far Just Like Heaven .
